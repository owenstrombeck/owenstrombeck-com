Bring Us the Stars

Bring Us the Stars was born during the pandemic when Sheri and I started collaborating with our longtime friends Julia and Kevin Keuler on a remote songwriting and recording project. Over the course of several months, we sent demo recordings of instrumental snippets as well as lyrical and vocal ideas back and forth in a shared google drive folder. We eventually iterated and adapted these ideas into a handful of songs and I started working on final instrument takes. We got together in-person at my home studio for a long weekend to record final vocal takes and a few weeks of mixing and mastering later - we had five finished songs for our first EP - ‘Are We Falling?’

I of course immediately had a list of ‘things I would do differently next time’ for the recording and production side of the project, and we collectively as a group had several more song ideas brewing, so we kept collaborating and over the course of the next several months created a second EP - ‘Smoke and Mirrors’.

These EPs are entirely self-produced and recorded in my home studio. I use a Behringer XR-18 as well as an Apollo Twin interface from Universal Audio for input A/D conversion. Vocals were recorded using a Lewitt LCT 440 Pure microphone - either in our pantry, or in the front living room closet that I emptied out and lined with acoustic foam for diffusion. Stringed instruments were direct-lined through the Apollo Twin and custom amp and effect simulation chains built in Guitar Rig. I used Cakewalk by Bandlab as my DAW, and a ton of free (and a few paid) VSTs for mixing and mastering.

Jordin Baas

I did remote session drum recording for two songs ('Sand' and 'Don't Take Your Love') for Jordin Baas's album 'Songs by a Human and a Tree'. Jordin sent me each song before final mixing and gave some direction. I recorded a few ideas while playing along with each and sent back options. After a couple rounds of feedback and input, I added some sound augmenting samples to the kick and snare tracks before sending final drum stems to Jordin. You can listen to the final results on Spotify.
